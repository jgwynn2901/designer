About Routing Plans

Routing Plans deliver claim information. Call Flows collect claim information.

The call representative uses call flows to obtain claim information from the caller. The call flow (A call flow is a group of screens that collect or lookup data as the call representative is taking the call.) requests claim information required to complete a particular type of call. It is specific to a line of business. For example the "Workers Compensation" call flow requests information required to correctly file a "Worker's Compensation Claim." The "Commercial Liability" call flow requests information required to correctly file a "Commercial Liability" claim.

Routing Plans are used to deliver claim information collected by call representatives. FNSNet places the claim information into a First Report of Injury claim form. This First Report Of Injury claim form is sent to destinations (A Destination is a place or person such as risk location, branch or caller.) by various methods (A call can be mailed, faxed or emailed.).

A Routing Plan (A Routing Plan has three parts: the type of transmission, destination and items sent.) is a plan for getting the claim to its destination.

A Routing Plan uses how, when and why information to get the claim to its destination. The type of transmission (Transmission Type is the type of transmission such as a fax, print, email, EDI, etc.) is the how information describing what way the information is sent. The destination address (Destination Address is the address of the destination such as fax number, a printer location or an email address.) is the where information telling to what place the information is sent. The output (Output is the items sent such as cover letter, first report of injury and overflow.) is the what information describing what is sent.

Example

Routing Plans are typically created in groups of 54. There is one routing plan for each line of business (auto, liability, and property) and one for every state and district of Columbia.
