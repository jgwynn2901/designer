Adding Transmission Sequence Steps

Transmission Sequence Steps are used to direct the transmission and contain transmission information such as destination, sequence and transmission type.

You have four choices in Adding Transmission Sequence Steps. If your transmission sequence step requires a large amount of original information, you may want to use New Transmission Sequence Step (New transmission sequence step creates an original transmission sequence step.). If your transmission sequence step requires a great deal of the same information found on other transmission sequence steps, you may want to use Copy This Transmission Sequence Step (Copy this transmission sequence step copies the transmission sequence step.).

If you want to update the transmission sequence step information you may want to use Transmission Properties (Transmission Properties enables you to edit transmission sequence step information.). If you want to erase a transmission sequence step use Delete This Transmission Sequence Step (Delete this transmission sequence step deletes the transmission sequence step.).

Click Navigation Details for information on how to get to Adding Transmission Sequence Steps.

Navigation Details

1. From the Navigation bar, choose Hierarchy.

2. Click the "+" sign next to FNS in the Hierarchy tree.

3. Select the client in the Hierarchy Tree to go to the Business Entity Summary page in the right frame.

4. Click the "+" sign next to the Routing Plans on the Business Entity page.

5. Select routing plan from list of routing plans and click "Edit" on the Routing Plans bar.

New Transmission Sequence Step

New Transmission Sequence Step

Right click the mouse on the routing plan in the Routing Plan details section and select "New Transmission Sequence Step" from the popup menu. Enter or select the New Transmission Sequence Step information.

  • Enter Destination string.

  • If applicable, check BATCH HOLD.

Batch Hold holds the claim and sends it later in a batch with other claims. It is typically used for special state print jobs and EDI.

  • Enter Alternate Destination String.

Alternate Destination String is typically used in the QA environment.

  • Enter Sequence, Retry Count and Retry Wait Time.

The Retry Count is three times for faxes and 1 time for printers.

  • Select Transmission Type from drop down list.

  • Click the "Save button".

Copy This Transmission Sequence Step

Copy This Transmission Sequence Step

Right click the mouse on the transmission sequence step on routing plan in the Routing Plan details section and select "Copy This Transmission Sequence Step" from the popup menu.

Transmission Properties

Transmission Properties

Right click the mouse on the transmission sequence step on routing plan in the Routing Plan details section and select "Transmission Properties" from the popup menu.

Update or change Transmission Sequence Step Properties. Click "Save" and "Close".
